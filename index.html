When Average Warming Becomes Daily Risk

A 1°C average increase can sound small. But what happens when that warming turns into more extreme hot days?

2020 is our baseline.

We start from 2020 and compare future CMIP6 projections through 2070. The first view shows average temperature change from that baseline.

Average warming is meaningful.

Even a small increase in average temperature shifts the climate that people, infrastructure, and ecosystems are used to.

But averages smooth over the days people actually feel.

A year can look only moderately warmer on average while still having many more days above dangerous heat thresholds.

Higher emissions can make extreme heat more visible.

Instead of only asking how much warmer a state becomes, we ask how many more extreme hot days people may experience.

Takeaway

Average warming helps us see the long-term climate shift. Extreme hot days help us understand how that shift may become part of daily life.
