03 · Future view

By 2070, the map gets warmer.

Under high emissions, average warming becomes visible across many states, even before looking at individual hot days.

04 · Hidden part

Average temperature smooths the real situation.

A yearly average blends comfortable days and extreme days into one number.
But people experience heat as specific days.

05 · Compare

Small average changes can hide larger daily changes.

Average temperature rises gradually, but the number of extreme hot days can grow faster. Counting days makes the risk easier to feel.

06 · Heat threshold

Count extreme hot days instead.

We count how many more days cross 35°C, turning average warming into a measure of daily heat risk.

07 · Uneven risk

Extreme heat days do not increase evenly.

By 2070, the largest increases appear in states that already sit near the 35°C threshold. The same average-warming future can create very different daily heat risks.

Takeaway

Average warming describes the climate. Extreme hot days describe the days people live through.

A small average change can still mean many more days above an extreme heat threshold. That is where climate change becomes daily risk.
