But averages hide the days you actually feel.

An annual average smooths out the days people remember — when school sports are cancelled, when AC runs all night, when outdoor work becomes dangerous.

Count the days that are hotter than normal instead.

Switching from average temperature to number of extreme hot days reveals a much sharper picture of the same future.

Averages look close. Extremes don’t.

Average warming can look slow and abstract. But when we count extreme hot days, the change becomes easier to see and feel.
