04 · Hidden part

Average temperature smooths the real situation.

A yearly average blends comfortable days and extreme days into one number.
But people experience heat as specific days.

05 · Translation

A few degrees can fill a calendar.

Average temperature change is measured in degrees, but daily risk is felt as extra days. Here, we translate average summer warming into additional 35°C+ summer days.

06 · Heat threshold

Count days above 35°C instead.

Here, a day above 35°C means the daily high temperature crosses 35°C. We count the delta in these days compared with observed 2020, turning average warming into a measure of daily heat-risk delta.

07 · Uneven risk

Days above 35°C do not increase evenly.

By 2100, the largest increases appear in states that already sit near this heat threshold. The same average-warming future can create very different daily heat risks.

Takeaway

Average warming delta describes the climate shift. Extreme hot-day delta describes the days people live through.

A small average-temperature delta can still mean many more days above an extreme heat threshold. That is where climate change becomes daily risk.
